Meeting 1: Discovery Session

Bridging the Manufacturer-Distributor Gap

Attendees: Richard Seaman (Wheelright), Keith Allen (Wheelright/Snider), Oliver Abrahamson (BigChange).

Wheelright is scaling rapidly with 30+ sites in the US and expanding globally. Your current "Teams and paper" approach is the primary bottleneck:

  • The Teams Data Trap: Manual reports are currently created by "data scraping" Teams messages—a process that is unscalable and prone to error.
  • Tribal Knowledge Risk: Component-level data (e.g., inverter voltage/PCBs) resides in people's heads rather than a serialized system.
  • Inventory Blindspots: No real-time visibility into "rolling stock" on distributor vans, leading to inefficient first-time fix rates.
  • Visibility Conflict: The need for Wheelright to see everything, while keeping Bridgestone and Michelin distributor data isolated.

Current State vs. Future State

Transforming Wheelright from manual communication to data-driven operations.

Current State (Manual & Disjointed)

  • Manual Math: Office staff spend hours weekly compiling performance data from chat logs.
  • Inventory by Luck: Distributors arrive on site without knowing if specific kits/parts are on the van.
  • Serialized Silos: Identifying specific hardware versions at a site relies on memory or searching emails.
  • No SLA Tracking: Difficulty proving response times across global distributor networks.

Future State (BigChange)

  • Automated Analytics: Real-time dashboards for Reactive Jobs, First-Time Fix Rates, and Site Performance.
  • Rolling Stock Control: Real-time van inventory management with automated reorder triggers.
  • Serialized DNA: Full history of every inverter, PCB, and sensor tracked to the specific serial number.
  • Super-User Visibility: Wheelright views all data while distributors only see their assigned sites.
